Curated fact sheet for the Portfolio Agent's corpus. Public facts only, grounded in

## Who Javier is

Javier Ramos Humeres is a full-stack software engineer with 4+ years shipping
production systems end to end, from requirements and architecture through backend,
frontend, and cloud. He is a physics graduate (B.Sc, Universidad Catolica) who moved
from astronomy into physics and then into software, and now builds with AI coding
agents. He is based in Santiago, Chile, and works in both English and Spanish.

## Career path

Javier started in astronomy, then physics, where he learned to model messy systems and
to trust fundamentals over hand-waving. He found he cared more about building than about
running experiments, so he moved into full-stack engineering.

At Pinflag he spent three years on the load-bearing parts of e-commerce: payment
integrations, reconciliation, order fulfillment, and analytics backends. That is where
he learned what production at scale really costs.

At TeselaGen he led a legacy-API migration to a modern NestJS clean-architecture
backend, and built the data and integration layers behind an AI copilot for biotech tools.
Around that work he began building an agentic-development toolchain: custom Claude
Code skills, MCP servers, and ticket-to-PR workflows.

## How Javier works

He ships production code daily with AI coding agents, using spec-driven and test-driven
development. Every feature starts as a written PRD, is implemented against it, and ships
only when CI is green and a peer has approved it. He pairs with teammates on system
design and on the urgent, unglamorous issues that keep a product healthy.

After shipping, he folds the lessons back into the toolchain: the repo context, the
skills, and the agent's memory, so the next session starts better prepared. This
portfolio is exhibit number one, built in public with that exact loop. He is currently
experimenting with autonomous loop workflows to push throughput and quality further.

## Agentic AI skills

Javier authors MCP servers (not just consumes them) and has shipped local RAG retrieval.
His agentic stack includes spec-driven development, multi-agent orchestrated workflows,
agent skills, custom Claude Code hooks, and verification loops. The Portfolio Agent
(this concierge) is his lead public flagship on that stack: LangGraph.js orchestration,
retrieval-augmented generation over pgvector, live tools, Langfuse tracing, and a
published evaluation. He works with Claude and other LLM providers through their APIs.

## Stacks and tools

Primary stacks: TypeScript and Node.js, NestJS on the backend, Next.js (App Router) on
the frontend, PostgreSQL with Prisma, and cloud deployment (Vercel and Railway here; AWS
in prior work). Agentic tooling: Claude Code, MCP servers, LangGraph, RAG on pgvector,
agent evaluation and observability with Langfuse.

## Beyond work

Javier is a former working musician (trombone) with a soft spot for where math, code,
sound, and craft overlap. He has a peer-reviewed publication on physics-based sound
synthesis in the Computer Music Journal (MIT Press). He builds isometric city-builder
games for fun and uses them as testbeds for agentic workflows. He cares about leaving
systems more honest than he found them.
